Implement a fused residual RMSNorm forward pass. Given two input tensors x and residual of shape (M, N), first add them elementwise, then normalize each row by its root mean square and scale each column by weight.

For each row \(m\) and column \(n\), compute:

\[ \begin{aligned} h_{m,n} &= x_{m,n} + \text{residual}_{m,n} \\ \text{rms}_m &= \sqrt{\frac{1}{N}\sum_{j=0}^{N-1} h_{m,j}^2 + \epsilon} \\ \text{output}_{m,n} &= \frac{h_{m,n}}{\text{rms}_m} \cdot \text{weight}_n \end{aligned} \]

Implementation Requirements

  • Use only native features (external libraries are not permitted)
  • The solve function signature must remain unchanged
  • The final result must be stored in the output tensor
  • Compute one independent RMS value per row

Example

With M = 2, N = 4, and eps = 1e-5:

\[ \begin{aligned} x &= \begin{bmatrix} 1.0 & 2.0 & 3.0 & 4.0 \\ -1.0 & 0.0 & 1.0 & 2.0 \end{bmatrix}, & \text{residual} &= \begin{bmatrix} 0.5 & -0.5 & 1.0 & -1.0 \\ 1.0 & 2.0 & -1.0 & 0.0 \end{bmatrix}, \\[8pt] \text{weight} &= \begin{bmatrix} 1.0 & 0.5 & 2.0 & -1.0 \end{bmatrix}, & \text{output} &\approx \begin{bmatrix} 0.5523 & 0.2762 & 2.9458 & -1.1047 \\ 0.0000 & 0.7071 & 0.0000 & -1.4142 \end{bmatrix} \end{aligned} \]

Constraints

  • 1 ≤ M ≤ 4,096
  • 1 ≤ N ≤ 1,024
  • All tensors use 32-bit floating point
  • eps = 1e-5
  • x and residual values are in [-3.0, 3.0]
  • weight values are in [0.5, 1.5]
  • Performance is measured with M = 4,096, N = 1,024
